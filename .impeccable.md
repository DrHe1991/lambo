## Design Context

### Users
BitLink targets a broad spectrum — from crypto-native Gen Z/Millennials to mainstream social media users being introduced to crypto, content creators seeking monetization, and investors looking for early-discovery returns. The common thread: they're on mobile, scrolling a social feed, and making quick financial micro-decisions (liking = investing). The context is casual-but-consequential — every tap has real monetary weight.

### Brand Personality
**Bold. Rebellious. Real.**

BitLink is the anti-establishment social platform where your taste earns you money. It's not another sanitized fintech app or generic DeFi dashboard. It has swagger — the confidence of early Bitcoin culture mixed with the addictiveness of a great social feed. It should feel like the cool thing your friend showed you, not something a bank built.

Emotional goals: **confidence** (trust it with your money), **excitement** (discovery and reward), **belonging** (you're early, you get it).

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Bold, high-contrast, opinionated. Not minimalist-to-a-fault, not childish/gamified. Somewhere between streetwear brand and premium social app.
- **Theme**: Dark mode default, light mode supported. Dark should be warm and tinted (not pure black), light should feel fresh and distinctive.
- **Colors**: Orange/amber is the brand accent. Avoid the generic "cyan-on-dark / purple gradient" crypto aesthetic. Tint neutrals toward warmth.
- **Typography**: Needs a distinctive font pairing — NOT Inter/Roboto/Open Sans. A bold display font for headings + a clean body font that's readable at small sizes on mobile.
- **Anti-references**: Generic DeFi dashboards (dark + neon + charts), childish gamification (badges, cartoon mascots, slot-machine UX), minimalist-to-empty interfaces.
- **References to channel**: The confidence of Cash App, the social energy of early Twitter, the editorial quality of Medium, the rebellious edge of crypto culture.

### Design Principles

1. **Every tap has weight** — This is a financial social app. Design should communicate that actions matter without making them feel heavy or scary. Clear feedback, honest microcopy, confident interactions.

2. **Distinctive, not decorative** — No gratuitous gradients, glowing blobs, or glassmorphism. Every visual element should earn its place. If it doesn't communicate something, remove it.

3. **Mobile-first, touch-native** — Designed for thumbs, not mice. Generous tap targets, swipe-friendly, no hover-dependent interactions. Fast, fluid, no jank.

4. **Warm rebellion** — Dark mode should feel warm and inviting, not cold and techy. The brand has edge but isn't hostile. Think "cool friend" not "hacker terminal."

5. **Trust through consistency** — Buttons look like buttons. The same action looks the same everywhere. Financial interfaces demand reliability — inconsistency erodes trust.
